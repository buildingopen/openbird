**FUNDAMENTO JURÍDICO / LEGAL BASIS**

The disruption to flight [FLIGHT NUMBER] constitutes a **cancellation** under Article 5 of Regulation (EC) 261/2004, as the original scheduled flight was not operated.

The airline's [rejection / non-response] is incorrect for the following reasons:

1. **Article 5 applies, not Article 6.** [IF RELEVANT: The airline's rejection cited "earlier arrival" as the basis for denying compensation. This argument applies to delay claims under Article 6, not to cancellation claims under Article 5. As the original flight was not operated, Article 5 governs.]

2. **The Article 5(1)(c)(iii) exemption does not apply.** The exemption from compensation requires the alternative routing to depart no more than one hour before the original scheduled departure. The routing I was placed on departed [X hours] before the original departure time, which does not satisfy this condition.

3. **[IF MARKETING/OPERATING CARRIER SPLIT:] Article 13 liability.** [MARKETING CARRIER] sold the ticket and is the marketing carrier. Under Article 13, the marketing carrier bears full passenger liability regardless of which carrier operated the affected leg.

4. **Article 9 care is owed regardless of cause.** Even if the airline were correct that extraordinary circumstances apply to Article 7 compensation, care costs under Article 9 are owed in all cases of cancellation requiring hotel accommodation. I am claiming €[CARE COSTS TOTAL] in actual documented expenses.
